The Boston Computation Club is a small reading club focused on mathematical computer science, and computational mathematics. Its name is plagiarized from the London Computation Club. Boston Computation Club meetings occur roughly every other week, on weekends, around 5pm EDT (modulo speaker availability). The format is a 20m presentation followed by 40m of discussion. Presenters can assume the audience is mathematically savvy, but cannot assume any definitions are known to begin with. The Club has no strict leadership but is gently guided by Max von Hippel, Jacob Denbeaux, & friends.
To join the Slack & attend future talks, or if you would like to give a talk, email vonhippel [ dot ] m [ at ] northeastern [ dot ] edu, or tweet @maxvonhippel.
